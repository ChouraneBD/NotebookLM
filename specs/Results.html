Q2. In a heterozygous individual (Aa), what is the probability of passing the recessive allele to an offspring?

expand_more

Your Answer: 25%

Genetics Medium

Your Answer

25%

Correct Answer

50%

auto_awesome AI Explanation

According to Mendel's Law of Segregation, during gamete formation, the two alleles for a trait separate from each other. A heterozygous individual (Aa) produces two types of gametes: 50% carrying the dominant allele (A) and 50% carrying the recessive allele (a). Therefore, the probability is 1/2 or 50%, not 25%.

Key Concept: Law of Segregation

"Alleles separate during gamete formation so that each gamete carries only one allele for each gene."

Q4. A keystone species is defined as:

expand_more

Your Answer: The species with the largest population.

Ecology Medium

Your Answer

The species with the largest population.

Correct Answer

A species with a disproportionately large effect on its environment.

auto_awesome AI Explanation

A keystone species is not necessarily the most abundant (that would be a dominant species). Instead, it is a species that plays a critical role in maintaining the structure of an ecological community, affecting many other organisms in an ecosystem. An example is the sea otter; without them, sea urchins would overgraze kelp forests.
